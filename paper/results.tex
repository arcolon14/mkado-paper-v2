\section{Application}

To demonstrate \mkado at genome scale,
we assembled a dataset of 12,437 human protein-coding genes
with both polymorphism and divergence data.
Codon-aligned orthologous sequences
for human (\textit{Homo sapiens}),
chimpanzee (\textit{Pan troglodytes}),
and orangutan (\textit{Pongo abelii})
were obtained from OrthoMaM v12 \citep{orthomam2024}.
Human intraspecific polymorphism was drawn from 178 Yoruba (YRI)
individuals in the 1000 Genomes Project NYGC high-coverage dataset
\citep{1000genomes2015},
yielding 356 phased haplotypes per gene.
Variants were mapped onto the OrthoMaM alignment coordinates
to produce multi-species FASTA files
suitable for direct input to \mkado.

Using chimpanzee as the outgroup,
genome-wide pooled counts were
Dn\,=\,37,120, Ds\,=\,65,228, Pn\,=\,44,850, and Ps\,=\,47,838.
The per-gene standard MK test identified 465 genes
with nominally significant departures from neutrality ($p < 0.05$),
though none survived Benjamini-Hochberg correction,
consistent with the limited power of single-gene tests
at human-chimpanzee divergence levels.
As expected,
the majority of testable genes showed NI~$>$~1 (\cref{fig:volcano}),
reflecting the well-characterized excess
of slightly deleterious nonsynonymous polymorphism in humans
\citep{eyre-walker2006}.
The aggregated asymptotic MK test estimated
$\hat{\alpha} = 0.075$ (95\% CI: $-1.22$ to $0.11$),
with the wide confidence interval reflecting uncertainty
in extrapolating the frequency-dependent $\alpha(x)$ curve
from the relatively shallow human-chimpanzee divergence.

Switching to orangutan as the outgroup
substantially improved precision.
The longer divergence time yielded roughly 2.5-fold more
fixed differences (Dn\,=\,94,578, Ds\,=\,188,325),
and the asymptotic estimate tightened to
$\hat{\alpha} = 0.163$ (95\% CI: 0.075 to 0.253),
indicating that approximately 16\% of amino acid substitutions
on the human lineage were driven by positive selection.
This estimate is consistent with previous analyses
of human coding sequences \citep{messer2013, eyre-walker2006}.

The entire analysis---standard MK tests,
asymptotic estimation,
and volcano plot generation for all 12,437 genes---completed
in under one minute on a multi-core workstation
using \mkado's parallel batch mode.
